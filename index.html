In\Visible St. Louis: A History of Eastern Missouri Alternative Sentencing Services

1991: EMASS Founded

EMASS, a private company offering alternative sentencing services to counties in Missouri, was founded by Michael L. Smith, a former state probation and parole officer with 17 years of field experience under his belt.

2000: EMASS and the St. Louis Courts

According to a St. Louis Post-Dispatch article written by Jennifer Mann in 2012, EMASS signed a contract in July 2000 with the St. Louis Circuit Court to become a provider of post-conviction supervision for misdemeanor defendants.

Although this contract explicitly included an option to extend the contract length after the initial three-year period, it was extended informally by virtue of the courts not taking action on this option. It also wasn't reviewed even as EMASS also began offering services other than those outlined in the initial contract (i.e. pre-trial supervision services).

2001: To Bail or Not to Bail?

Missouri's Department of Corrections brought pre-trial supervision programs to an end. In response to this, some jurisdictions in Missouri just established their own programs but the St. Louis Circuit Court decided to partner with EMASS to provide supervision. As a result, EMASS filled this gap between an initial charge and a defendant's trial for St. Louis County.

2010: Alcohol Monitoring Contract

EMASS was awarded a contract from the Missouri Department of Corrections / St. Louis County Justice Services to oversee a new alcohol monitoring program.

2012: St. Louis Post-Dispatch Article

In 2012, Jennifer Mann of the St. Louis Post-Dispatch wrote an article that presented information about EMASS that included the views of judges, bail bondsmen, and actual defendants.
